Annexure

Maturity Framework

The PMDT classifies organisational maturity across five levels based on a scoring scale of 1 to 5:

Level Description Score
Level 1: Non-existent No practices or systems are in place. 1.0
Level 2: Informal Ad hoc practices that are specific to the person, team, and/or situation. Inconsistent and dependent on individual initiative rather than organisation-wide systems. 2.0
Level 3: Enabled Policy processes, capability, and support systems are in place. Conditions are created for implementation, but usage may still vary across teams or functions. 3.0
Level 4: Practiced Formal systems and practices are enacted, generally effective but requiring concerted effort to embed. Requires concerted effort and management support to sustain and deepen adoption. 4.0
Level 5: Embedded Systems and practices are part of culture, used consistently and confidently, with success. Regular review drives continuous improvement. 5.0

Maturity Classification Scale

Based on the average scores reported across functions, departments are classified as follows:

Score Range Maturity Level
0 – 1.0 Non-existent
1.0 – 2.0 Informal
2.0 – 3.0 Enabled
3.0 – 4.0 Practiced
4.0 – 5.0 Embedded
